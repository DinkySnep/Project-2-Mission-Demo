The sound I chose to add was a woosh that plays when the ball is launched.
I made two changes to make the game cooler, the first was to add a new material type, rather
than every building being out of the stone I added a wood material. This is visually different and
is 4x lighter in the physics engine so it's easier to move with the ball. This creates a new level of
difficulty as to how you can play around the two different materials. Each level has been
augmented to have this new material. I have also added a star score system that gives the
player a goal to work towards. You are able to achieve a maximum of 4 stars throughout playing
the game. The stars are given at the end of the game based on how many balls you used to
reach each goal. Each level is possible to complete in a single ball throw (I have done this twice
and my one playtester was also able to do it). Five tosses for the five levels is how you get 4
stars, if you do ten or less you get 3, if you do 20 or less you get 2 and everything else only
rewards one star. This adds replayability to the levels so that players could work to get all the
stars in the game by changing how they choose to take on each level. The last level features a
small touch of randomness as the way to get a single ball shot can sometimes be obscured ever
so slightly by the clouds meaning depending on your luck you may not immediately see the
secret to getting a hole in one on your first playthrough.
